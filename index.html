When the Cosmos Lost Its Authority
They told me the stars were ancient, that they were the oldest beauty we know, burning for millions of years just to be seen for a second. ㅤ Then I met her, and suddenly the sky felt… outdated. ㅤ Astronomers spend lifetimes decoding constellations, naming distant fires, mapping cold brilliance that cannot feel the hands that worship it. ㅤ But her eyes, her eyes do not need telescopes. They do not live light-years away. They look back. They respond. They understand. ㅤ Stars collapse in on themselves. They die spectacular deaths and call it glory. She, on the other hand, creates life in silence, with a laugh that rebuilds tired hearts, with a voice that steadies gravity in my chest. ㅤ The sky is admired because it is unreachable. She is extraordinary because she is near. Because her beauty is not a performance for distant observers. It is something you feel in the marrow. ㅤ The stars glitter like scattered diamonds, but they flicker, tiny, trembling things fighting the dark. She does not flicker. She remains. Even in her doubt, even in her quiet moments, there is a steady radiance that makes galaxies feel insecure. ㅤ If the universe could see her, it would rearrange itself. Constellations would unlearn their patterns. Supernovas would dim politely. The night would apologize for ever thinking it was magnificent. ㅤ Because the truth is, the stars are just distant fires burning alone. ㅤ She is warmth. She is gravity. She is the reason I look up and realize ㅤ the most breathtaking thing in existence isn’t above me. ㅤ It’s her.
